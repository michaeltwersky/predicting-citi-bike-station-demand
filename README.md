# Forecasting Citi Bike Station Demand to Improve Efficiency of Operational Rebalancing

## Introduction

To address the operational bottleneck of rebalancing stations during rush hours, this study forecasts bike demand for Citi Bike stations in New York City.

## Goal

My work aims to train a reliable machine learning model that accurately predicts bike demand between neighborhood clusters in Manhattan and Brooklyn. This model can be used to improve current rebalancing operations down to the 15-minute time interval.

## Description of the Data

Obtained directly from Citi Bike, NYC weather stations, and the City of New York's NYC Open Data Project, the data consists of seven files:

- `202408-citibike-tripdata_1.csv`: ride data for the month of August, 2024

- `202408-citibike-tripdata_2.csv`: ride data for the month of August, 2024

- `202408-citibike-tripdata_3.csv`: ride data for the month of August, 2024

- `202408-citibike-tripdata_4.csv`: ride data for the month of August, 2024

- `202408-citibike-tripdata_5.csv`: ride data for the month of August, 2024

- `weather_data_2024.csv`: historical weather in NYC

- `Borough_boundaries_20251104.csv`: NYC borough boundaries by latitude and longitude

Download all files from the [v1.0 release page](https://github.com/michaeltwersky/applied-ml-project/releases/tag/data)
